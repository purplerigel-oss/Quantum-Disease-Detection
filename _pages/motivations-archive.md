Plants are highly important resources that play a variety of roles globally, including nourishing, clothing, and enriching the lives of the human population. However, especially with the lack of genetic diversity in the commercial farming industry, it is highly common for crops to suffer from disease and blights. The unique utilization of quantum algorithms and machine learning makes it possible for disease to be spotted quicker than ever, which increases net yield for farmers, resources available worldwide, and understanding of the predictors of disease. I aim to improve my understanding on the benefits of said utilization and how these benefits are achieved.

Additionally, most resources on the subject of quantum machine learning related to plant disease detection combine different technologies and circumstances in their views, which makes it difficult to see the whole picture. For example, multiple different quantum algorithms and systems have been innovated and used in this field (ex: Quantum Behaves Particle Swarm Optimization, Quantum Circuit Born Machine, Quantum Fourier Transform, Variational Quantum Circuits, and Quantum Whale Optimization Algorithm) - each study using a slightly different method to achieve their results. The goal is to synthesize all available information and provide an overview to a still developing field.
